Case Study: Phishing Attack on a College Student

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source. It usually happens through emails, messages, or fake websites that look genuine.

A college student named Rahul received an email that appeared to be from his bank, State Bank of India. The email claimed there was suspicious activity in his account and asked him to verify his details immediately. Step-by-step, the attack worked like this: first, Rahul clicked on the link provided in the email; second, he was redirected to a fake website that looked exactly like the bank’s official page; third, he entered his login ID, password, and OTP; finally, the attackers used this information to access his account and transfer money.

Phishing attacks commonly target students, elderly people, and individuals who are less aware of cybersecurity practices. Students are especially vulnerable because they frequently use online services and may not always verify the authenticity of messages.

The consequences can be serious. Rahul lost a significant amount of money, faced stress and anxiety, and had to go through a lengthy process to report the fraud and secure his account. Such incidents can also lead to identity theft and misuse of personal data.

This case highlights the importance of being cautious, verifying sources, and never sharing sensitive information through unknown links or messages.
